**Selected Mode:** Guided

**Techniques Available:**
- MDA Framework Exploration

- Anti-Game Design
- Physics Playground
- Toy Before Game

**Focus Areas:**
- Transforming real-world map data into a playable GTA 3 style city space
- Designing a satisfying third-person driving experience as the initial core interaction
- Defining the player fantasy of exploring, driving, causing chaos, and eventually hijacking and combat
- Balancing real geographic fidelity with fun, readable, game-friendly level design
- Building a browser-first architecture that supports high performance on desktop
- Creating an extensible asset and rendering pipeline for upgraded textures, models, and world props
- Identifying the minimum viable sandbox that proves the concept early
- Anticipating procedural generation failures, map data gaps, and edge cases from OpenStreetMap
- Preserving visual appeal and gameplay density in generated environments
- Planning for future expansion from driving prototype to full open-world systemic gameplay

**Recommended Technique Combinations:**
- Core Loop Brainstorming + Verbs Before Nouns + Game Feel Playground
- MDA Framework Exploration + Player Fantasy Mining + Emotion Targeting
- Constraint-Based Creativity + Accessibility Layers
- Failure State Design + Emergence Engineering
- Genre Mashup + Environmental Storytelling

**Why These Combinations Fit This Project:**
- They prioritize a fun browser-playable prototype before full feature scope
- They help distinguish the game's identity from GTA while preserving the sandbox fantasy
- They directly address procedural generation risk and performance constraints
- They support a phased path from driving sandbox to richer open-world systems

## Session Summary

### Most Promising Concepts

**Top Pick: Driving-First Real-Place Sandbox**
A free, open-source, browser-based GTA-inspired sandbox where the player enters any real location, spawns directly into a reliable starter car, and immediately explores a compact but faithful slice of that place through satisfying third-person driving. This stands out because it combines the strongest emotional hook from the session with a realistic v1 scope and a clear long-term expansion path.

**Runner-up: Truth-Preserving Simplification**
The world should stay as close as possible to OpenStreetMap road structure, scale, terrain, and district rhythm while simplifying geometry and visual detail only where necessary for readability and performance. This is promising because it protects the core "this feels like my place" fantasy better than chasing higher-fidelity art too early.

**Honorable Mention: Stable City, Living Session**
Reloading the same location should produce the same core roads, buildings, and spawn area while allowing traffic and pedestrians to vary between sessions. This is worth exploring because it gives the game both repeatable place identity and fresh sandbox energy without undermining recognition.

### Key Insights

- Real-place recognition is the emotional center of the game.
- Driving feel is the first and most important proof point.
- Roads, intersections, scale, traffic, and elevation matter more than high-detail visuals.
- A small, browser-performant, driving-first sandbox is the right v1 scope.
- The game should stay pure sandbox and low-friction rather than leaning on progression systems.
- Long-term extensibility should focus on replaceable assets and smarter generation rules.
